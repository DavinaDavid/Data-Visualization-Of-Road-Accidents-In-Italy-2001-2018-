Evolution of Road Accidents in Italy (2001–2018)
Project Overview

This project analyzes road accidents in Italy from 2001 to 2018, focusing on trends over time, regional distributions, and vehicle involvement. The goal is to uncover patterns in accident frequency, identify high-risk areas, and understand which vehicle types contribute most to accidents.

Primary Research Question:

How did road accidents and vehicle involvement evolve in Italy from 2001 to 2018?

Supporting Sub-Questions:

Which year recorded the highest number of accidents?
Which regions experienced the most accidents annually?
Which macro-areas (North, Center, South) show the highest risk?
Which vehicle types contributed most to accidents over the years?
Data
Source: MIT Open Data (Italy Ministry of Infrastructure & Transport)
Dataset: Road accidents by vehicle type and region (2001–2018)
Format: CSV/XLS
Coverage: 20 regions, aggregated into macro-regions for analysis
License: CC-BY 4.0

Data Quality Notes:

Some years show unusual spikes (e.g., vehicle involvement in 2015)
Missing values were handled and replaced with 0 for numeric accuracy
Dataset translated from Italian to English for consistency
Methodology

Data Preparation:

Cleaned and formatted data in Power BI and Excel
Standardized region names and created macro-region aggregation
Converted accident counts to numeric and handled missing values

Analysis Techniques:

Time-series trend analysis (2001–2018)
Regional and macro-region aggregation
Comparative analysis of vehicle types and accident trends

Tools Used:

Power BI (data cleaning, processing, and visualization)
Excel (data preparation)
ChatGPT (guidance and documentation support)
Key Insights
Trend Over Time: Road accidents decreased from ~263,000 in 2001 to ~173,000 in 2015. Vehicle involvement spiked in 2015 due to reporting changes.
Peak Year: 2002 had the highest accident count (~265,000 incidents).
Regional Distribution: Lombardia recorded the highest total accidents (737,166), while Valle d’Aosta had the lowest (6,192). Northern Italy contributed the largest share overall.
Macro-Region Analysis: Northern Italy remains the riskiest region, followed by Southern & Central regions.
Vehicle Contribution: Private cars (especially with trailers) were the leading contributors to accidents, followed by motorcycles and bicycles. Larger vehicles like buses and trams contributed less.
